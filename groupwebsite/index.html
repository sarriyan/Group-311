Yogurt is a dairy product that is created by fermenting milk with specific bacterial cultures. The primary bacteria used in yogurt production are Lactobacillus bulgaricus and Streptococcus thermophilus. These bacteria convert the lactose in the milk into lactic acid, which thickens the milk and gives yogurt its characteristic texture and tangy flavor.

Do you know about the benefits of yogurt to humans? Yogurt is a rich source of probiotics, which are beneficial bacteria that promote a healthy gut microbiome. These probiotics can aid in digestion and boost the immune system. Different cultures around the world have their own variations of yogurt, such as Greek yogurt, Icelandic skyr, and Indian dahi. These varieties can vary in taste, texture, and nutritional content.
